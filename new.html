An Autobiography

The Story of My Experiments with Truth," is a remarkable literary work that delves into the life of one of the most influential figures in modern history, Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi. Throughout the book, Gandhi reflects on his personal and political evolution, the values and principles that guided his life, and his unwavering commitment to non-violence, or "Satyagraha." He discusses his experiments with truth, simplicity, and self-discipline, which became the cornerstones of his philosophy and leadership.

Key Details About the Autobiography

  • Genre: Autobiography
  • Publication Year: 1927
  • Language: Originally written in Gujarati, later translated into various languages
  • Chapters: Divided into multiple chapters covering different phases of Gandhi's life
  • Themes: Non-violence (Satyagraha), Truth, Simplicity, Self-discipline
  • Significance: One of the most important autobiographical works of the 20th century
